The history of Eritrea is rich and complex, marked by a struggle for independence and cultural resilience. Eritrea was colonized by Italy in the late 19th century and remained under Italian control until World War II.

After the war, Eritrea was federated with Ethiopia in 1952 but was annexed by Ethiopia in 1962, leading to a long and brutal war for independence that lasted nearly 30 years. Eritrea finally gained independence in 1993, following a successful armed struggle against Ethiopian forces.

Since independence, Eritrea has focused on nation-building, fostering a strong sense of identity and community among its diverse ethnic groups. The capital, Asmara, is a testament to this rich history, showcasing Italian colonial architecture and vibrant culture.

Today, Eritrea stands as a symbol of resilience and determination, continuously working toward development and unity among its people.
